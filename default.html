About Me

Hey there! My name's Jack Ding. It's nice to meet you. A 60-second introduction: I'm a second year computer science student at the University of Western Ontario. I'm really fortunate to have found a major that I'm truly, genuinely passionate about. Computer science gave me the freedom to test my creativity and critical thinking both in and out of class. I look forward to developing more projects in the years to come.

Coffee gets me through the day, and tea through the night. In my spare time, I like to sing, debate, and watch TED Talks.






Programming & Design

One aspect of tech that I'm constantly fascinated with is the intersection of programming and design. I've always held the belief that software should be as beautiful as it is functional. I'm glad that the notion of design has evolved past the idea of simply "making things pretty". Google's Material Design language is just one of countless examples indicating this progression.

Something I've always been curious about is how traditional design influences future design. We've seen how Swiss-style graphic design principles influenced the rise of Flat UI. We're also starting to see examples of hand-drawn illustration over vector-based graphics in web and mobile. I'm interested to see how tech design changes in the coming years.
